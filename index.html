A Quick "About Me"

I grew up in the 90's playing street hockey, riding bikes, and drinking out of garden hoses all day. By the early 2000's, I began my hacking journey by writing custom scripts for Counter-Strike and HTML for my Xanga, MySpace, and chat forum posts. After years in school bands and rock bands, I started a journey in audio engineering that led to work on lots of projects, including some pretty big ones like EA Sports UFC 2 and Cyberpunk 2077. I used this craft as a mental model for software engineering concepts, helping me transition into my first techie role where I successfully launched a Seattle nonprofit's first web application in 2017.

Nowadays, I am a frontend lead that cofounded and maintains an inner-source UI library along with an internal React application. In my spare time, I read about the craft of software engineering and various concepts around efficiently delivering value in the field.

"It's 2025, why does your website look like this?"

Great question! I'll expand on this later, but the TLDR is that I'm focusing on semantic HTML and web accessibility while I write a few libraries that can progressively enhance this website without compromising either one. There are existing solutions that solve this problem, but these libraries/frameworks come and go. Plus, it's much more fun and beneficial to write my own!
